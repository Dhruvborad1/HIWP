Understanding Ayurvedic Body Types

Your Prakriti (constitution) determines your ideal approach to health and wellness

Vata

Characteristics: Creative, energetic, thin build, dry skin, quick mind

Balancing Foods: Warm, nourishing, moist foods; cooked grains, soups, nuts

Exercise: Gentle yoga, walking, tai chi

Daily Routine: Regular schedule, warm oil massage, calming activities

Pitta

Characteristics: Intelligent, driven, medium build, warm body, sensitive skin

Balancing Foods: Cooling foods; sweet fruits, vegetables, dairy

Exercise: Moderate activities, swimming, cycling

Daily Routine: Avoid overheating, moonlit walks, cooling practices

Kapha

Characteristics: Calm, strong, solid build, smooth skin, methodical mind

Balancing Foods: Light, warm, spicy foods; legumes, vegetables, honey

Exercise: Vigorous activities, running, aerobics

Daily Routine: Early rising, dry brushing, stimulating activities

Personalized Diet Plan

This diet plan is customized for your Pitta-Kapha constitution. Focus on cooling foods that balance Pitta and light foods that stimulate Kapha.
Recommended Foods
  • Fruits: Sweet berries, apples, pears, melons, grapes, mangoes
  • Vegetables: Cucumber, zucchini, broccoli, cauliflower, leafy greens
  • Grains: Barley, oats, basmati rice, quinoa
  • Proteins: Mung beans, tofu, chicken (in moderation), freshwater fish
  • Dairy: Milk, ghee, fresh yogurt (in moderation)
  • Spices: Coriander, fennel, mint, turmeric, cumin
Foods to Avoid
  • Fruits: Sour fruits like oranges, grapefruit, sour berries
  • Vegetables: Tomatoes, eggplant, spinach, radish, hot peppers
  • Grains: Corn, rye, brown rice (in excess)
  • Proteins: Red meat, seafood, eggs
  • Dairy: Aged cheeses, sour cream, salted butter
  • Spices: Chili peppers, garlic, mustard seeds, excessive salt
